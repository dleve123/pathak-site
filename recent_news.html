• Recent News

  • We have organized a very exciting and affordable Cancer Imaging workshop to be held in beautiful Austin this fall. So please encourage your students/post-docs/faculty to attend so that they can learn about the very latest in pre-clinical and clinical cancer imaging.

  • Heartiest congratulations to both Stacy Gil and Meghana Kalavar for being awarded the Provost's Undergraduate Research Award for 2014. Stacy is going to develop an educational tool for teaching students about the neurovasculature and Meghana is going to use multiphoton microscopy to study neurovascular decoupling in disease. We are very proud of you both!

  • The Pathak Lab's new article on 'Multiscale and Multimodal Imaging of Angiogenesis in Breast Cancer' has been featured on the cover of the July issue of Angiogenesis. This is the first report describing the integration of multiple imaging methods to examine blood vessel remodeling during breast cancer progression. Fantastic job Jana Cebulla, Eugene Kim and Jiangyang Zhang!

  • Dr. Pathak has been invited to give the weekend educational lecture on Tumor Angiogenesis at the upcoming annual meeting of the International Society for Magnetic Resonance in Medicine in Milan, Italy. Dr. Pathak has also been invited to chair the session on Preclinical and Clinical Tumor Therapy Response.

  • Dr. Pathak has been invited to give Spotlight Lecture and Chair the Spotlight Session on Multiscale Imaging at the upcoming annual meeting of the World Molecular Imaging Society in Seoul, South Korea.

  • The Pathak Lab's new article in collaboration with Dr. Popel's Systems Biology Lab on simulating blood flow in breast cancer based on imaging data has been featured on the cover of Microvascular Research. This is going to be the journal's cover for the entire year! This is a huge honor so congrats all and special thanks to Spyros Stamatelos and Eugene Kim for making this possible!
